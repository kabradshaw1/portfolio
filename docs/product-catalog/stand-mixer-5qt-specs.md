### DC Motor Advantage

DC motors in stand mixers provide several benefits over traditional AC motors:
- **Quieter operation:** DC motors produce less mechanical noise at equivalent power
- **Consistent speed:** Maintains selected speed under load (heavy doughs won't slow the motor)
- **Energy efficiency:** More efficient power conversion, especially at lower speeds
- **Longer lifespan:** Brushless DC design eliminates brush wear

## Speed Settings

| Speed | Setting | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Stir | Speed 1 | Folding delicate ingredients, starting dry additions |
| Speed 2 | Speed 2 | Slow mix, combine wet and dry without overmixing |
| Speed 3-4 | Medium-Low | General mixing, batter, cookie dough |
| Speed 5-6 | Medium | Creaming butter, cake batter |
| Speed 7-8 | Medium-High | Beating eggs, thick frostings |
| Speed 9-10 | High | Whipping cream, meringue, thin icings |

### Speed Selection Tips

- **Always start on low (Stir)** when adding dry ingredients to prevent flour clouds
- **Increase speed gradually** for creaming; starting on high prevents proper aeration
- **Meringue:** Start at Speed 4 until soft peaks form, then increase to Speed 8-10 for stiff peaks

### Dough Hook

- **Purpose:** Kneading yeast doughs — bread, pizza, pasta, bagels
- **Best for:** Any dough requiring gluten development through kneading
- **Use:** Mix ingredients on Speed 1-2 until combined, then knead on Speed 2-3 for 8-12 minutes
- **Note:** Do not exceed Speed 4 with dough hook; excessive speed can overheat dough
- **Material:** Die-cast metal with powder coat finish
- **Dishwasher safe:** Yes

### Wire Whip

- **Purpose:** Incorporating air into ingredients — whipped cream, meringue, light frostings
- **Best for:** Whipping heavy cream, egg whites, mayonnaise, light cake batters
- **Note:** Do not use wire whip with heavy doughs; it can bend the tines
- **Material:** Stainless steel wire with die-cast hub
- **Dishwasher safe:** Yes

## Care Instructions

### Daily Care

- Wipe the motor body with a damp cloth after use
- Do not submerge the motor body or get water in the ventilation slots
- Wash bowl and attachments in warm soapy water or dishwasher (top rack)
- Dry all parts completely before reassembly

### Periodic Maintenance

- Oil the tilt-head hinge mechanism annually with a small amount of food-safe mineral oil if it feels stiff
- Check the worm gear (accessible from beneath the head) every 2-3 years; regrease if dry
- Clean the bowl-lock groove periodically — dough buildup can prevent proper locking

### Common Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Motor slows under heavy dough | Normal on Speed 1-2; switch to Speed 3 for better torque |
| Bowl wobbles during mixing | Ensure bowl is fully seated and twisted to lock position |
| Head won't stay down | Adjust the head tension screw (located inside the head hinge area) |
| Flour splash on startup | Always begin mixing on Stir (Speed 1) before increasing speed |
